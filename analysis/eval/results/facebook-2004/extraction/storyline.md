## What is thefacebook.com?

thefacebook.com is an expanding online directory that connects students, alumni, faculty and staff through social networks at colleges and universities. It allows user connections on the basis of friendship, courses and social networks (including intra and inter-school networks), and has a built-in messaging system.

## User Profiles

Each user maintains a profile that includes:
- Contact information
- Personal information: relationship status, political views, clubs, jobs, and favorite music, books, movies and quotes
- Course information: built-in database of school courses and concentrations that automatically builds a class schedule
- A picture

Additionally, profiles automatically pull in links to school news articles that reference the user, the user's last AIM away message, and the user's last access location (using a built-in database of school dormitories and halls).

## Social Networking

**Intra-school networking:** Users can browse their school's social network through:
1. Social Net -- displays ten random people from the user's school
2. Course Rosters -- displays all students enrolled in any given course
3. Advanced Search Engine -- allows search on every user profile parameter

Users can add friends to their profile (pending second-source verification). The site automatically visualizes each user's friendship network and shows the connection between any two users (friends, friend's friends).

**Inter-school networking:** Users can browse other schools through global search and maintain separate friendship lists for each school in the network.

## School Expansion

Launched February 4, 2004 at Harvard University. By April 2004, the network included 12 schools: Stanford (Feb 26), MIT (Mar 14), NYU (Mar 21), Boston University (Mar 21), UC Berkeley (Apr 4), Duke (Apr 11), Georgetown (Apr 11), UVA (Apr 11), Tufts (Apr 19), Boston College (Apr 19), Northeastern (Apr 19), University of Illinois (Apr 19).

The mission is to expand to include most schools in the United States. By September 1, 2004, the network will have more than 200 member schools.
