# Shared constraints for layout description

You are analyzing a presentation slide image. Your job is to produce a **layout description** — a textual fingerprint of the slide's spatial structure, NOT its content.

## What to capture

- **Layout type**: title-only, title+subtitle, title+bullets, title+body, two-column, image-heavy, data-table, chart/graph, full-bleed-image, quote, section-divider, closing/CTA, timeline, comparison, icon-grid, etc.
- **Spatial zones**: where content blocks sit (top/center/bottom × left/center/right), approximate proportions
- **Element inventory**: what kinds of elements exist (heading, subheading, body text, bullet list, numbered list, image, icon, chart, table, shape, logo, divider line)
- **Visual weight distribution**: where the visual mass concentrates (top-heavy, centered, left-heavy, balanced, etc.)
- **Whitespace pattern**: minimal, moderate, generous, asymmetric

## What to IGNORE

- Actual text content (words, sentences, names, numbers)
- Specific colors or brand identity
- Font names
- Image subjects (describe as "large photo", "icon cluster", "chart", not what the photo shows)
