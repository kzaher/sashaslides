# Peloton Brand Positioning

## Target Consumer: Serious About Fitness

**Psychographics:**
- Prioritize fitness
- Work out 4+ times per week
- Enjoy the energy of workout classes
- Follow an exercise routine with multiple types

**Demographics:**
- Spend $150+ USD/month on fitness
- Typically live in big suburban areas
- 35-50 years old (average 43)
- $100-$150K+ household income
- Typically married with kids
- Highly educated

## Consumer Promise

- **Reasons to Believe:** An integrated experience designed to pull you in and ensure you literally show up
- **Functional Benefit:** Entertainment and competition to show up at your best on your terms
- **Emotional Benefit:** A transformative experience so you show up as your full self all day long

## Brand Values

- **Always On** -- gold standard for being there for members
- **Empowering** -- lifting members up through celebration
- **Optimistic** -- inspirational, energizing, hopeful and hyped-up
- **Captivating** -- blends performance with entertainment
- **Authentic** -- member-first, simple, conversational
- **Premium** -- established through design, performance and excellence

## Who We Are / Who We're Not

**Who we are:** Motivating, Modern, Premium, Authentic, Enthusiastic, Captivating, Confident, Inclusive, Street Wise, Empowering, Optimistic, Community.

**Who we're not:** Preachy, A Fad, Exclusive, Sterile, Over the top, Goofy, Aggressive, Cultish, Snobby, Religion, Satisfied, Cheap, A Fitness Brand.

## Anti-Patterns

- No before-and-after photos
- No cheesy ads
- Not a fad
- Don't talk down to customers

## Brand Wheel

At the center: Peloton "Makes You Want To Show Up."

**Product truths:** Leaderboard and Metrics, Passionate Connected Community, Top Tier Instructor Talent, Always Ready.

**Functional Inward:** Makes Hard Work Fun, Removes Barriers, Pushes Me to Excel.

**Functional Outward:** Innovative Design, Wholly Immersive, Engineered for Competition.

**Emotional Inward:** Like Nothing Is In My Way, Like I Belong to Something, Like I Can Accomplish Anything.

**Emotional Outward:** Captivating, Tech-Savvy, Discerning, Committed.

## Creative Evolution

The brand storytelling has evolved from product-led to emotional-led advertising:
- 2014-2016: "This is Peloton" / "Morning Ride" (product-focused)
- May 2017: "Hello, Let's Go" (transitional)
- Holiday 2017: "Give it All" (more emotional)
- Spring 2018: "Better in Us" (fully emotional)

## Brand Pillars in Creative Execution

**Makes Hard Work Fun** -- The brand is about making hard work enjoyable. Visually, this means heroeing content and instructors while still always showing hard work (not a party on a bike).

**Pushes Me to Excel** -- Metrics, the leaderboard and engaging content keep you motivated. Creative always shows intensity and protagonists working hard.

**Innovative Design** -- Creative dramatizes the beauty of the bike's design and technological advancements, always shown in real-life settings (not sterile product shots).

**Wholly Immersive** -- Marketing always shows the protagonist fully engaged and immersed in class, not distracted. This is their escape. Content is the hero.

**Committed** -- Creative shows the work and the intensity to reinforce that Peloton users are wholeheartedly dedicated.
